# Video-type guides

## Interview or conversation

Organize by question and topic. Preserve meaningful differences between speakers, follow-up reasoning, personal experience, and explicit uncertainty. Do not merge conflicting views into one consensus.

## Tutorial or software demonstration

Organize by prerequisites, setup, operations, parameters, expected results, common errors, and recovery steps. Inspect interfaces, code, and visual results. Turn the demonstrated sequence into a reproducible checklist.

## Lecture or course

Organize by learning objectives, definitions, principles, derivations, examples, exercises, and connections to earlier material. Explain prerequisite concepts when the speaker relies on them.

## Business or market analysis

Organize by background, problem, evidence, assumptions, model, judgment, strategy, risks, and leading indicators. Preserve data dates and distinguish forecasts from observations.

## Opinion or commentary

Organize by thesis, premises, supporting arguments, rhetorical examples, counterarguments, boundary conditions, and conclusion. Flag unsupported leaps without turning the note into a debate essay.

## Documentary or case study

Organize chronologically where causality depends on sequence. Separate observed events, interviews, narration, and later interpretation.
